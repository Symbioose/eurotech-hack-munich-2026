# Jury Audience Context - Smart City Track

This note summarizes the likely evaluation lens of two Smart City judges based on public LinkedIn profile information shared by the team.

It is not competitor intelligence. It is audience context to make the pitch clearer, more relevant and more credible.

## Tsvetan Iliev

Profile signal:

- Automotive Program Manager at Sensata Technologies.
- Founder of Vinghen, an all-terrain e-scooter / micromobility company.
- Experience across vehicle sensing, controls, embedded engineering, suppliers, manufacturing, certification and B2B deployment.
- Built hardware from prototype toward real-world deployments.
- Public posts emphasize supplier reliability, manufacturing delays, compliance, product design quality, safety and disciplined execution.

Likely evaluation lens:

- Is the hardware story credible, or just a nice UI?
- Does the team understand sensors, enclosure, power, RF, durability and manufacturing risk?
- Is the scope disciplined enough for 48 hours?
- Are the supplier / GBA claims concrete, or generic?
- Does `Apply Fix` show a real engineering constraint, not a cosmetic change?

What to emphasize for this profile:

- We are not claiming final CAD or certified engineering.
- Physical Cursor generates the first reviewable hardware brief.
- BuildGuard includes deployment constraints: facade, humidity, rain, typhoon exposure, battery, RF and maintenance access.
- The demo warning is a real hardware/deployment issue: weatherproofing / IP rating / protected membrane / corrosion.
- The GBA route separates electronics, enclosure/metal, pilot integration and compliance/logistics.

Risk if we pitch badly:

> If we sound like "ChatGPT generates hardware", the feasibility claim becomes easy to question.

Defense line:

> We do not replace hardware engineers. We move them from blank-page discovery to reviewing a concrete device concept, BOM, risk map and RFQ.

## Pitch Implications

The pitch must satisfy both lenses:

1. **Hardware credibility**
   - one real DfMA/deployment warning
   - one visible fix
   - honest technical guardrail: first reviewable brief, not final CAD

2. **Startup / Hong Kong credibility**
   - concrete buyer
   - clear budget owner
   - why Hong Kong
   - why GBA
   - repeatable business model

## Recommended Opening

Use this opening:

> Hong Kong has thousands of aging buildings, and mandatory inspections are periodic. Between inspections, owners and inspectors often do not know what is changing. Physical Cursor turns that dense-city problem into a reviewable smart-city hardware brief: deployment context, 3D BuildGuard Node, sensor graph, BOM, weatherproofing risk fix and GBA supplier route.

Why it works:

- addresses Hong Kong immediately
- stays Smart City
- shows the physical device
- does not overclaim manufacturing
- prepares the technical and business story

## Lines To Avoid

Avoid:

- "We generate final CAD."
- "We solve manufacturing."
- "Anyone can manufacture hardware now."
- "BuildGuard replaces building inspectors."
- "We have real supplier quotes."
- "This is a marketplace first."
- "We built a generic world model."

Use instead:

> We generate the first reviewable hardware brief: deployment context, 3D node, BOM, risk map, RFQ and supplier route.

## Judge-Specific Proof Points

For a hardware/program-management judge:

- show the warning and fix clearly
- say why the fix matters
- show BOM and RFQ changed after the fix
- keep scope honest

For an entrepreneurship/ecosystem judge:

- show buyer and route to pilot
- say why Hong Kong accelerates this project
- explain GBA supplier network as expansion layer
- close with repeatable business model
